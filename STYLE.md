# STYLE
Base style guide: **Microsoft Writing Style Guide**.

## Voice
- Confident and direct. State what the tool does; skip the hedging.
- Technical but plain. Explain mechanism (regex, NLI model, hook surface) without jargon for its own sake.
- Evidence-backed. Claims about AI tells cite the measurement, not vibes.
- Second person ("you", "your project"). Contractions are fine.

## Formatting
- Headings in sentence case.
- Close up em-dashes: `word—word`, no surrounding spaces.
- Code, commands, and file paths in backticks.
- Expand an acronym on first use (LLM, NLI, CLI).
- Use a list when items are parallel; use a sentence when they're really one thought.

## Forbidden phrasings
- Cliché openers ("In today's fast-paced world…", "In the ever-evolving landscape of…").
- Manufactured contrast ("not just X — it's Y", "not only… but…").
- Vague attribution ("studies show", "experts say") without a citation.
- Reflexive hedging ("it's important to note that", "it could be argued that").
