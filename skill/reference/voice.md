# voice — inject brand voice and personality into flat copy

The "could be any product" problem: technically fine prose with no personality. Anchor it to
the project's actual voice.

## Flow
1. Read PRODUCT.md (voice in three words + anti-references) and sample the project's best
   existing writing. Hold both as the target.
2. Run the detector first and strip the generic slop (Family A) — you can't add voice on top of
   buzzwords.
3. Rewrite the flat passages to carry the project's voice: its rhythm, its diction, the way it
   addresses the reader. Use the anti-references as a "don't sound like this" guardrail.
4. Check fidelity against PRODUCT.md — voice is consistency with a defined personality, not
   random flourish.

## Guardrails
- Don't manufacture a voice the project doesn't have; amplify the one it does.
- Personality is not jokes-per-paragraph. Restraint reads as confidence.

Leans on: Family A (remove generic) first, then voice injection against PRODUCT.md.
