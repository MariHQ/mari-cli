# soften — tone down hype and overclaiming

The mirror of `sharpen`: prose that oversells. Replace puffery with specific, verifiable claims.

## Flow
1. Run the detector; read the buzzword / exclamation / contrast findings.
2. Rewrite, in order:
   - replace superlatives and puffery with a concrete, checkable statement ("blazing-fast" →
     "renders in under 100ms")
   - cut marketing buzzwords (`marketing-buzzword`: "revolutionary", "seamless", "empower")
   - drop manufactured contrast (`manufactured-contrast`: "not just X — it's Y")
   - calm the punctuation (`exclamation-overuse`) and the legacy/significance boilerplate
     (`significance-boilerplate`)
3. Keep the claim true and modest. If you can't back a superlative with a fact, cut it rather
   than soften it to a vaguer superlative.

Leans on: `marketing-buzzword`, `exclamation-overuse`, `manufactured-contrast`, `significance-boilerplate`.
