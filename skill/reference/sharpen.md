# sharpen — make timid, hedged prose direct and confident

The opposite of overclaiming: prose that won't commit. Cut the hedges and let the claim stand.

## Flow
1. Run the detector; read the hedge/passive/nominalization findings.
2. Rewrite, in order:
   - cut reflexive hedges (`hedge-overuse`: "it could be argued that", "it's worth noting")
   - convert passive → active (`passive-voice`), naming the actor
   - turn zombie nouns back into verbs (`nominalization`: "make a decision" → "decide")
   - delete weasel words (`weasel-word`: "fairly", "somewhat", "arguably")
   - kill expletive openers (`there-is-expletive`)
3. Keep claims the author can stand behind — sharpening commits to the existing claim, it
   doesn't inflate it into a bigger one. If a hedge is load-bearing (a real caveat), keep it.

Leans on: `hedge-overuse`, `passive-voice`, `nominalization`, `weasel-word`, `there-is-expletive`.
